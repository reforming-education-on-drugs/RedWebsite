Our Constitution

Article 1: Name

The name of the organization shall be RED (Reforming Education on Drugs) Club, hereinafter referred to as RED.

Article 2: Mission

RED aims to provide in-class educational sessions regarding drug abuse prevention. Through the means of interactive scientific demonstrations and presentations, we will establish a foundational understanding of the biological mechanisms involved in drug addiction and the social implications involved.

Article 3: Membership

Membership will be open to all students at the University of Calgary, and to other members of the community. A valid membership will be recognized for the term September to August. The club will have a minimum of 20 members at all times to remain in operation, 2/3 of whom shall be active members of the Students’ Union. The club will collect a minimum $5.00 membership fee per individual member each year. As a member, an individual is able to be considered for all leadership opportunities presented by the club, as well as in class presentations in middle schools.

Benefits and Rights: Furthermore, by joining RED, members are able to directly contribute to the well-being and education of younger students in their own communities. Members, also have prime opportunities to practice and perfect their presentation and communication skills, develop researching skills, and enhance their ability to communicate complex ideas in simpler terms. Members can attend meetings, participate in club events, run for executive positions, and all such activities. Executive Members may also participate in voting on decisions and planning of events.

Termination of Membership: Membership may be revoked if a member resigns or is asked to resign via an impeachment (Article 6). Executive members wishing to resign must provide their resignation in writing to all other members of the executive two weeks prior to their intended date of resignation.

Article 4: Students’ Union

RED acknowledges and will abide by the Students’ Union Bylaws and Policies (unless otherwise approved by the Club Committee). We are aware the RED will cease to be a Students’ Union club should a Policy or Bylaw be violated without Club Committee ratification.

Article 5: Office and Duties

A. Co-President

  • Coordinate all operations of club and ensure they abide by Students’ Union Policies and Bylaws
  • Serve as a co-signing authorities for club financial transactions.
  • Work with other executive members to coordinate volunteering opportunities and partnerships with Elementary and Junior-High Schools.
  • Seek out and maintain mutually beneficial partnerships with on campus services, businesses and organizations, as well as after school service providers in the city.
  • Organize fundraising opportunities and events
  • Ensure that day-today operations are running efficiently

B. Vice President External

  • Facilitate the communication between Elementary and Junior-High Schools with RED
  • Coordinate presentation dates, communicate club objectives
  • Ensure that the presentations meet the criteria/requirements for the school(s)
  • Responsible for brand development and public relations

C. Vice President Internal

  • Communicate club meeting schedules, promotions, and other such events with club members
  • Track volunteer hours contributed by club members
  • Track meeting minutes and create meeting agendas
  • Keep an up-to-date list of active members

D. Vice President Finance

  • Responsible for all club financial matters, including acting as a co-signing authority for club financial transactions.  
  • Coordinate club purchases and funding requests. 
  • Submit the Annual Report to the Students’ Union
  • Maintain financial records

E. Vice President Technology

  • Development of website
  • Maintenance of website
  • Creation of website content
  • Logos and advertisement creation

F. Director of Education

  • Responsible for the fine-tuning of presentations
  • Ensures that presentations truly represent the clubs philosophy
  • Ensures that presentations are interactive and understandable
  • Ensures and maintains a high standard of presentation delivery
  • Receives feedback from schools (students and teachers) to improve presentations
    (Relay concerns with Director of Research and Development)

Junior Vice President of Marketing and Public Relations

  • Regularly provide event updates and postings on various social media websites
  • Assist VP Information Technology with development of content

Junior Vice Presidents of Education

  • Work with members and the Director of Education to train and prepare club members for in-class presentation
  • Organize and manage the presentation schedule
  • Lead presentation practice sessions

Junior Vice President of Sponsorships

  • Work alongside the VP Finance to assist and lead in the acquisition of funds
  • Organize fundraising events; coordinate members, coordinate times and schedules

Article 6: Executive Terms of Office

All executive positions will be appointed (please see Article 7 for specifications). The term of office shall commence on May 1st and end on April 30th of the following school year. Both executive and general members must provide 2-weeks resignation notice. Resigning executive members are responsible for the transitioning of a new executive member, this new member will be appointed by the co-presidents and remain in office for the remainder of the term.

Any member may be impeached for the following:

  • Mismanagement of finances
  • Failure to maintain responsibilities
  • Criminal/illicit behaviour (consistent)

Prior to official impeachment, a meeting must be held between the executive member in question and the rest of the executive team. This will serve as an opportunity to address the issues at-hand, and hear reasons from all executive members. If impeachment is pursued, then another executive meeting must be held in order to come to a final decision; this will require a 2/3 majority vote of the sitting executive team (excluding the executive in question).

Article 7: Incoming Executives

Executive positions will be advertised to all club members as they become available. All members will have the equal opportunity to apply for any open executive position.

  1. The election of a new club Executive will take place given a vacancy of said position.
  2. All applicants will be required to submit a resume and statement of intent
  3. All applicants will be interviewed by at least 2 of the sitting executive members
  4. An executive meeting will be held to discuss the qualification of all applicants
  5. A 2/3 majority vote by the executive team will be required to appoint an applicant to the vacant position
  6. Appealing Election Results: The individual behind this movement must present their reasons of appeal before the newly elected and past executive members. This committee of individuals will then vote on whether to disregard the appeal or re-vote for the specified position(s), depending on the reasons and evidence presented.
  7. Proxy votes are allowed, under the following conditions: The member representing the absent member must have the decision (regarding the subject) signed and written by the absentee.

Article 8: Transition of the Executive

The sitting executives are responsible for teaching the incoming executive(s) on club policies and procedures. The new executive(s) will also be presented with club HUBS as well as custodianship of club records, materials, and bank/financial records. The outgoing executive is responsible for all year-end activities including the submission of the Annual Report to the SU, locker clean-out, and preparing of financial statements.

Article 9: Meetings

Any executive member may call an emergency meeting, with at least 24 hours notice. There shall be at least one Executive meeting and/or General Meeting per month during the academic year. Presence of 2/3 of Executive members shall constitute quorum. It is the executive duty of both the co-presidents to chair all meetings; however, this can be done individually if one of the co-presidents is not present. In a scenario in which both Co-presidents are unable to attend, any other executive member is expected to take-over. It is the role of Vice President Internal to record meeting minutes, notify club members of meetings and meeting agendas, and to make these records and agendas available to all members.

Article 10: Amendments

This constitution shall comprise the operating basis of RED. All amendments to this document must be ratified by a two-thirds vote of the club membership. A copy of the amended constitution must be submitted to the Students’ Union within two weeks of ratification
